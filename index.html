Idle Party Dungeon Privacy Policy

Effective date: 15 July 2026

Idle Party Dungeon is developed and published by BroglioGames. This policy explains how the game handles information.

Information handled by the game

The game stores gameplay progress and settings locally on your device. A randomly generated save profile identifier and a shortened one-way hash derived from the device identifier are included in save metadata to identify save versions and resolve sync conflicts. They are not used for advertising or tracking.

Google Drive cloud saves are optional. If you choose to connect Google Drive, the game requests only the drive.appdata permission. This allows the game to create and access its own hidden application-data folder in your Google Drive. The game cannot browse or access your other Drive files. Cloud-save data contains game progress, settings, save identifiers, timestamps, and conflict-resolution metadata.

Google handles the Google Account sign-in flow. Idle Party Dungeon does not receive or store your Google password. On Windows, the authorization token is encrypted locally using device-bound information. On Android, authorization is handled by Google Play services.

How information is used

Information is used only to operate the game, save progress, sync an optional cloud save, restore progress, and resolve save conflicts. The current version does not include advertising, analytics, crash-reporting, or profiling SDKs. BroglioGames does not sell personal information.

Google processes account authorization and cloud data under Google's own privacy terms. No other third-party service receives game data from the current version.

Storage, retention, and deletion

Local game data remains on your device until you clear the app's data or uninstall it, subject to your platform's backup behaviour. Google Drive app data remains in your Google Account until you delete the hidden app data through Google Drive's app-management settings.

You can disconnect Google Drive at any time from Settings → Google Drive Cloud Saves. Disconnecting revokes the game's authorization and removes its locally stored authorization state, but it does not delete the cloud-save file already stored in your Google Drive app-data folder.

Children

Idle Party Dungeon is a general-audience fantasy game and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. The optional Google Drive feature is controlled by the user's Google Account and Google's age and supervision rules.

Security

Reasonable technical measures are used to protect locally stored authorization data and cloud communications. No storage or transmission method can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

Changes

This policy may be updated when the game's features or legal obligations change. The effective date above will be updated when material changes are made.
